Construction law in Uganda governs the legal framework for planning, designing, and executing building projects. It intersects with environmental law, land use, occupational safety, and contract enforcement. Legal practitioners play a critical role in ensuring that infrastructure development is safe, compliant, and sustainable.

üìò Core Legal Instruments

  • Building Control Act, 2013: Establishes the National Building Review Board and mandates building permits, inspections, and compliance with national codes.
  • Building Control Regulations, 2020: Operationalizes the Act with detailed procedures for plan approvals, accessibility standards, and penalties.
  • Physical Planning Act, 2010: Declares Uganda a planning area and requires development permission from local Physical Planning Committees.
  • National Environment Act, 2019: Requires Environmental and Social Impact Assessments (ESIAs) for projects with potential environmental risks.
  • Occupational Safety and Health Act, 2006: Ensures worker safety on construction sites.

  • Non-compliance: Building without permits or deviation from approved plans attracts fines and demolition orders.
